BUSINESS CASE
================================================================================

Liebherr Australia carries $46M in spare parts inventory to support 214 mining
equipment units across Queensland and Western Australia. Standard inventory
management (2.0× annual turnover) ties up excessive working capital in
slow-moving stock.

Implementing ABC classification with differentiated stocking policies—4× turns
for critical drivetrain components through vendor-managed inventory, 6× turns
for consumables through central distribution—reduces inventory to $23.7M while
maintaining service levels.

METHODOLOGY TRANSPARENCY
================================================================================

This analysis models 25 representative critical spare parts across excavators,
haul trucks, and dozers, covering drivetrain, hydraulics, electrical systems,
undercarriage, and wear components. These parts represent typical criticality
distribution for mining equipment fleets.

Total inventory values are scaled using industry benchmarks: OEM spare parts
inventory typically represents 1.5-2.5% of revenue for heavy equipment
manufacturers (Caterpillar, Komatsu, Hitachi benchmark data). At 2.0% of
Liebherr Australia's $2.3B revenue, total baseline inventory is estimated
at $46M.

The ABC optimization methodology and 48.4% improvement are derived from the
representative 25-part sample and applied to scaled totals. The improvement
percentage is methodology-driven (differential turnover rates by criticality
class) rather than dependent on absolute inventory size.
